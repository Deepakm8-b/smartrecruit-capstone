Executive Summary
The report contains Software Requirements Specification (SRS) and Project Management Plan (PMP) of Smart Recruit which is a Smart Student - Organisation Recruitment Platform. The service will operate on the basis of aligning university graduates with the employment firms in Australia, through AI-based job matching, career guidance, recruiter referral services, and industry training workshops. The Smart Recruit platform helps to fill one of the major gaps in the Australian graduate employment market. Students are finding immense challenges with opportunities that are relevant to their skills and organisations are having difficulties in sourcing good entry-level talent effectively. Smart Recruit is responding to this by becoming a four in one business that is a job board, career coaching service, recruitment agency and a training provider. The site makes revenues in two forms namely, student Premium subscriptions, and organisation placement commissions, based on 10-20 percent of the first year salary of a successfully recruited student. The safe method of payments is through PayPal. The system is supposed to be written in PHP, MySQL, HTML, CSS and JavaScript awaiting final directive of the supervising lecturer. Every stage of the project (planning, development, and non-functional and functional requirements, system architecture, database design, wireframes, risk management, project schedule, and responsibilities) are covered in this document. 
1. Introduction
1.1 Project Overview
Smart Recruit is a web based recruitment process which is set to bridge the gap between hiring organisations and the university students. The site enables the students to build detailed profiles, receive job recommendations based on skills, apply to jobs that match them and get organization career advice. The process of registering, posting job listing, viewing skill matched candidates and paying a placement 
commission can only be effected once a Smart Recruit-referred student has been successfully recruited. The project is a personal capstone project under development in ICT307A in the Excelsia University College, Sydney. The platform will target addressing the gross gaps identified in the process of reviewing the existing recruitment platforms, including Seek, LinkedIn and Indeed.
1.1.1 Competitor Analysis and Identified Gaps
"A competitor analysis is a structured approach to identifying the strengths and weaknesses of existing platforms in the same market, with the goal of identifying opportunities to deliver superior value to users (Porter, 1980)."
Three key recruitment websites (Seek, LinkedIn and Indeed) were analyzed in terms of user feedback and features to determine gaps in the existing offering to student and graduate job seekers in Australia.

	•	Lack of transparency and feedback - the users submit lots of applications but are not informed why they have been rejected and how they can improve their applications.
 • Poor job matching - The job recommendations are not made properly considering the skills of the user or career objectives, especially in the specialised jobs in IT and technical areas. Lack of structured career guidance - the users are not assisted in helping them to understand what skills they need to learn to help them be job ready. 
High level of reliance on hiring agencies- the result is poor communication, responsibility and less connection between the students and the employers. 
None-identified skill gap platforms do not make the users aware of the existing gaps in skills and how to cope with them.
1.1.2 How Smart Recruit Addresses These Gaps
SmartRecruit particularly bridges the abovementioned gaps by offering career preparation, rather than job posting. The platform offers a skill-based recommendation system which matches students jobs based on their verified skills profile, identifies required skills, and offers a clear career plan of the steps the student needs to take to obtain the desired job. It allows interaction between students and organisations directly and eliminates the excessive use of recruiting agencies. Also, SmartRecruit ensures institutional email addresses are verified to ensure the authenticity of the profiles, which also increases the trust between hiring organisations and students. The project is feasible in the context of its scope of development since it concentrates on a number of main functionalities: user authentication, profile management, job posting, skills based matching with rule-of-thumb logic and simple recommendation output. The platform is implemented with a rule-based matching system, rather than complex machine learning, and it can be implemented within the scope of a one-developer capstone project with typical web technologies such as PHP, MySQL, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript (Sommerville, 2016).
1.2 Product Scope
Smart Recruit has four areas of core services. The first is matching student job matching whereby the system uses AI to match student profiles with job postings based on skills, education, and career interests. The second one is the career guidance whereby premium students are provided with individualised career roadmap, skills gap analysis, one-on-one coaching, reference letter and digital certificates. The third is the recruiter referral service whereby the platform owner personally refers qualified premium students to partner organisations. The fourth one is training sessions, in which premium students will be able to browse and book face-to-face or online training events provided by industry partners.

2. Requirements Engineering
Requirements engineering may be defined as the process of identifying, documenting and managing the needs and expectations of stakeholders in a software system. Sommerville (2016) defines requirements engineering as the process of eliciting, analysing, validating and managing the requirements that define what a system should do and the constraints within which a system should operate. In the case of SmartRecruit, the requirements engineering process entailed an analysis of the needs of three main stakeholders, namely, students, organisations and the platform administrator.
2.1 Identification of Actors
An act in a use case diagram is a role that a user or other system or hardware takes when interacting with the system under modelling (Pressman and Maxim, 2020). There are four main actors of the SmartRecruit platform, which interacts with the system. The Student is a university student who signs up, creates a profile, applies to jobs, and is optional on the Premium tier. The Organisation is a hiring company that registers, posts job listings, views candidates and pays a commission on successful hires. The Admin is the owner of the platform who controls all the users, approves posts, issues referrals, and monitors operations. The Payment Gateway (PayPal) refers to an external system that handles all financial transactions such as subscriptions, bookings and commissions.

2.2 Identification of External Entities, Boundaries and Interfaces
A system boundary helps to determine what falls within the system and what falls outside (Yourdon, 1989)." The system boundary of the SmartRecruit system is defined as all the server-side PHP processing, the relational database MySQL, the front-end HTML/CSS/JavaScript and the PayPal payment integration layer. All components within this boundary are built and maintained by the platform. External entities are outside this boundary and will deal with the system using specified interfaces. 
. The internal systems of the hiring organisations, university enrolment databases or third-party training providers are not considered to be part of the system boundary. In the current version, student enrolment verification is self-reported. The development and testing phase is carried out using the PayPal Sandbox environment, which is used to simulate all the payment transactions, until the platform is ready to be deployed in production.

3.3 Architectural Pattern
The Model-View-Controller (MVC) is a software architecture pattern that divides an application into three major sections: the Model, which contains data and business logic; the View, which is in charge of the user interface; and the Controller, which processes input and orchestrates between Model and View (Fowler, 2003).
 SmartRecruit is based on the MVC architecture style. The Model layer handles all database interactions using PDO prepared statements, and imposes business rules like calculating match scores and validating subscriptions. View layer will render the HTML templates with dynamic data sent by the controller, which will cleanly separate the presentation and logic. The Controller layer processes all of the HTTP requests, manages the user sessions, does input validation, and routes requests to the right model and view components.

6. Assumptions, Dependencies and Concerns
6.1 Assumptions
The assumptions used in planning and development of SmartRecruit platform are the following.
 The students can use the platform with a modern web-browser and a stable internet connection. 
• Organisations are registered and have a valid Australian Business Number (ABN) in Australia.
 • All AUD transactions are secured with PayPal. SmartRecruit does not keep any payment cards information on the platform.

Payment processing: This will require the accessibility of the PayPal API. In case PayPal goes offline, any payment capabilities will be disabled in the meantime. 
The matching algorithm of jobs requires that students must have complete and up to date skill profiles in order to generate meaningful match scores. 

6.3 Constraints
The current version of the SmartRecruit platform has the following constraints.
 • The prototype is meant to be deployed on single server and is not yet configured to support horizontal scaling and load balancing.

 Mobile responsiveness is optimised to screens with 768px and over. The smaller devices will be fully mobile optimised during Phase 2.

Focus on the niche graduate and university student market that large platforms ignore. Continue improving the skill-based matching algorithm. Build university partnerships that large competitors cannot easily replicate.
R14
Legal liability
Engage a legal advisor before platform launch to draft terms and conditions. Include clear commission and referral agreements signed by organisations at registration. Obtain professional indemnity insurance before going live.

7.3 Risk Evaluation
According to the above risk analysis, data breach (R02), organisation commission disputes (R03), and privacy compliance (R08) are the three highest-priority risks to the SmartRecruit platform. These risks are classified as High because they may have serious operational, financial or legal impacts. Proactive mitigation strategies have been implemented on all of them, as part of the system design and operational processes. R02 is addressed by using BCrypt hashing and by using PDO prepared statements constructed into the PHP codebase. Mitigation on R03 is achieved by well written commission terms at the time of organisation registration. R08 is alleviated by publishing a privacy policy and data minimisation practices.
